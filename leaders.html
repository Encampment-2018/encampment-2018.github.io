Schedule

For Deacons, camp runs May 29 through June 2. Deacons should arrive at camp on Tuesday, May 29, between 5 PM and 7 PM. Teachers and Priests will check in at camp the following afternoon (May 30). Wards must clear campsites no later than noon on Saturday.

Arrival at Camp


  1. Upon arrival, you will be greeted at the property gate by a staff member. His job is to make sure that your first impression of camp is a good one and that all your initial needs and questions are satisfied. He will direct you to your assigned campsite. Once your equipment is unloaded, drivers will return their vehicle, including trailers, to the parking area near the gate. No more than one vehicle and enclosed trailer are allowed to remain at your campsite.

  2. All other vehicles are to be parked in the designated area near the entrance gate. The scoutmaster will report to the medical cabin for check in. Bring the following to check-in your ward:

    • Troop roster
    • BSA swim check form for youth and adults
    • BSA medical form parts A, B, and C for each youth and adult
    • Troop medication form
    • Signed parent permission form for each young man
    • Signed Horsemanship Waiver

  3. The senior Patrol Leader will oversee camp setup.
  4. After setup, the scoutmaster should spend some time with the deacons reviewing the schedule provided by the Stake. If there are any major conflicts between badges they really wanted, the scoutmaster should to talk to the Stake. If not already done, deacons should fill out merit badge blue cards at this time.

Packing List

Scouts should travel to camp wearing their standard BSA Field uniform (Class A). On Wednesday all should wear a Camp T-shirt. On Thursday, all should wear their troop activity uniform (Class B). On Friday all should wear their standard BSA Field uniform (Class A). Activity appropriate footwear is required at all times while at camp except when swimming. Campers are encouraged to bring shoes which cover the entire foot. Flip flops and sandals are not allowed.

Recommended for Troop:

  • First aid kit
  • US flag
  • Troop flag
  • Patrol flags
  • Bulletin board
  • Service project equipment & materials
  • Battery powered clock
  • Table(s) and other gear required for meal preparation
  • Portable gas fire pit
  • Shade structre(s)
  • Trash collection container
  • Drinking water containers

Recommended for Leaders:

  • Alarm clock
  • Advancement records
  • Scoutmaster handbook
  • Latern(s)
  • Merit badge blue cards
  • Current merit badge packets
  • Clothesline
  • Camp paperwork
  • Contact information for parents of scouts
